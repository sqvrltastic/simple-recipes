Crepes

Also known as blini or palaƒçinke; they are similar to pancakes, but 100% better.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium-sized eggs (eggs prevent them from tearing)
  • approximately ¬æ liter of milk, add more if needed
  • white all-purpose flour, add just enough so that the batter has the consistency of a runny yogurt
  • pinch of soda
  • 3 tsp of sugar
  • pinch of salt
  • 1 tbsp of refined sunflower oil, heated up on a pan, then dumped back into the batter and mixed together

Directions:

Mix all ingredients in a large bowl until you get a homogenous batter.

Set your stove on high-ish heat and place a non-stick pan for crepes, wait a few minutes until it heats up.

Scoop and pour batter onto the pan. Swirl it around. Try to add just enough batter to cover the pan, but don't add any more than that. Ideally, they should be pretty thin, but it can be tricky to get right first few times, so don't feel bad if your blin tears.

Wait until the corners start to turn slightly caramel and a few bubbles appear on the surface. This is your cue to try and flip the blin on the other side. Use silicone or wooden spatula (don't use metal utensils on not-stick pan). Gently try to stick your spatula under the blin and then flip it over. Ez.

Place your blin on a plate and rub it gently with a bit of butter (NOT FUCKING MARGARINE) so it doesn't stick to other crepes that you can then stack on top of each other. ü•û
